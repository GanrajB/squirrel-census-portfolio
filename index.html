2018 Central Park Squirrel Census

A Data Analytics Portfolio Project

Exploring squirrel behaviors and spatial patterns in Central Park, NYC

Project Overview

This project analyzes the 2018 Central Park Squirrel Census dataset, capturing observations of squirrels in New York City's Central Park. The dataset includes details on squirrel behaviors, physical characteristics, and spatial-temporal data.

Key objectives include cleaning the dataset, performing exploratory data analysis (EDA), and creating visualizations to uncover patterns in squirrel behaviors and distribution. This project showcases skills in Python, Pandas, Seaborn, Folium, and statistical analysis, tailored for data analytics roles.

Key Findings

Behavioral Patterns

Foraging and eating are the most common behaviors, with juveniles showing higher foraging activity compared to adults. Gray squirrels dominate in chasing activities.

Spatial Distribution

Squirrels are concentrated in specific plots, with higher activity in southern Central Park. Choropleth maps highlight these hotspots effectively.

Statistical Insights

Weak correlation between plot number and ground sighting measurements. AM shifts show slightly higher activity than PM shifts.
